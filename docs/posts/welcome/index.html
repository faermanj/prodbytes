Welcome to Production Bytes!

2026-06-25

Notes on enterprise software delivery, from vibe-coded to well-architected. Practical takes on shipping systems that hold up in production, and how to avoid the failures that get you paged. For people who care about getting it to production and keeping it there.

Why this, why now

Shipping software has never been easier to start, and never been harder to get right. AI can scaffold an app in an afternoon. A prototype that demos beautifully can be in front of stakeholders by lunch. But the gap between “it works” and “it works in production, under load, for years, without paging someone at 3 a.m.” is as wide as it’s ever been — maybe wider.

That gap is where most of the pain lives, and almost nobody writes about it honestly.

We’re at a strange moment. Teams are vibe-coding their way to working prototypes faster than ever, while the disciplines that actually keep systems alive — reliability, observability, governance, sane deployment, knowing what your code does when it’s 2 a.m. and the dashboards are red — get treated as afterthoughts. The result is a growing pile of software that ships fast and breaks quietly.

Production Bytes exists to close that gap. Not with theory or vendor talking points, but with practical notes on enterprise software delivery: how to take something from vibe-coded to well-architected, and how to avoid the production nightmares that turn a good launch into a bad quarter.

Why now? Because the cost of getting this wrong is rising. More of the world runs on software that touches money, health, and high-stakes decisions — and “move fast and break things” doesn’t survive contact with an audit, an outage, or a regulator. The teams that win from here aren’t the ones who ship fastest. They’re the ones who ship fast and can stand behind what they shipped.

What kind of community are we?

Production Bytes is for people who actually ship. We’re collaborative — we care about each other and help each other through the hard parts, sharing what worked, what broke, and what we’d do differently. We’re deeply technical — real architecture, real trade-offs, real failure modes, no buzzwords. And we’re focused on delivery — getting projects to production and keeping them running there.

If you build software that has to work, and you want to get better at it alongside people who feel the same way, you’re in the right place.
